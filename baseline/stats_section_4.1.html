Section 4.1: Overall Performance Summary — Full Details

Overview

Section 4.1 provides the definitive CytoSig vs SecAct summary across 6 datasets at each dataset’s primary independent level. This supplement includes all aggregation levels (not just the primary independent level shown in the main report). Four method rows per dataset:

  •  CytoSig  — all 43 cytokine signatures (33–43 passing filters per dataset)
  •  CytoSig (matched)  — restricted to the 32 targets shared with SecAct (27–32 per dataset)
  •  SecAct  — all 1,170 secreted protein signatures (805–1,161 passing filters)
  •  SecAct (matched)  — restricted to the 32 targets shared with CytoSig (27–32 per dataset)

The matched rows enable apples-to-apples comparison: both methods evaluated on the exact same 32 targets (22 direct name matches + 10 alias-resolved via ALIAS_MAP).

Independence-Corrected Methodology

All correlations use a median-of-medians approach to ensure statistical independence:

  • GTEx (by_tissue, 29 tissues): Each target’s representative ρ = median of its per-tissue Spearman ρ values across 29 tissues.
  • TCGA (primary_by_cancer, 33 cancer types): Same median-of-medians approach across 33 cancer types.
  • CIMA, Inflammation Atlas Main, scAtlas Normal, scAtlas Cancer: Each target has one ρ at the donor/tumor level (no tissue/cancer stratification needed), so no median-of-medians aggregation is required.

% Sig: Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction (q < 0.05) applied within each dataset × method group.

Composition effect revealed: On the same 32 shared targets, SecAct (matched) outperforms CytoSig (matched) in all 6 datasets: GTEx (0.442 vs 0.257), TCGA (0.560 vs 0.327), CIMA (0.302 vs 0.173), Inflammation Atlas Main (0.438 vs 0.352), scAtlas Normal (0.579 vs 0.375), and scAtlas Cancer (0.505 vs 0.291). Even in Inflammation Atlas Main — the only dataset where full SecAct (0.173) appeared to trail full CytoSig (0.323) — matched SecAct (0.438) exceeds matched CytoSig (0.352), demonstrating that the apparent CytoSig advantage was a composition effect from full SecAct including many low-signal targets that dilute its median.

Matched improvement: Restricting to shared targets improves both methods: CytoSig (matched) median ρ exceeds CytoSig (total) in all 6 datasets (e.g., GTEx 0.257 vs 0.151, +70%), confirming that the 32 shared targets are among the best-performing cytokines.

Supplementary Aggregation Levels

The primary independent levels above represent the statistically rigorous comparison. Below are additional aggregation levels shown for completeness. These fall into two categories:

  • Pooled levels — aggregate across tissues or cancer types, inflating correlations through confounding (e.g., GTEx donor_only pools 29 tissues per donor).
  • Finer cell-type levels — stratify by cell type within donors, typically reducing correlations as pseudobulk groups shrink and noise increases.
